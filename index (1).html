Welcome to SPIN Selling

What is SPIN?

SPIN is a questioning methodology that moves prospects from awareness to action:

  • Situation: Understand their current state
  • Problem: Identify pain points
  • Implication: Amplify the cost of inaction
  • Need-Payoff: Get them to articulate why they need your solution
Why SPIN Works: People believe what THEY say, not what YOU say. SPIN gets THEM to make your case.

Week 1: Foundation (Days 1-7)

Weekly Goal

Master Situation & Problem questions. Learn to identify specific pain points.

Days 1-3: Situation Questions Only

Your Mission

Learn to research and ask great Situation questions.

  • 20 calls per day (ONLY Situation questions)
  • Research 10 companies each morning (5 min each)
  • Write down 3 Situation questions for each company
  • Take detailed notes in CRM
  • Listen 70%, talk 30%
Core Situation Questions

General:

  • "Walk me through your current process for sourcing electronic parts."
  • "Who's involved in purchasing decisions at your company?"
  • "How many suppliers do you typically work with?"
  • "What system do you use to manage inventory?"

CEM-Specific:

  • "How many production lines are you running?"
  • "What's your typical lead time from PO to delivery?"
  • "Do you handle box-build or focus on PCBA?"

OEM-Specific:

  • "How many active products do you have in production?"
  • "What's your process when a part goes EOL?"
  • "Who handles component engineering versus purchasing?"

Days 4-7: Add Problem Questions

The Bridge: Situation ‚Üí Problem

Use what you learned from Situation questions to naturally transition:

"You mentioned you work with 8 suppliers... that makes me curious ‚Äì what's the biggest challenge with managing that many relationships?"
Core Problem Questions

Supply Chain Problems:

  • "When a part goes on allocation, what happens to your production schedule?"
  • "How often do you encounter parts that are hard to find?"
  • "Have you ever missed a delivery deadline due to parts shortages?"

Cost Problems:

  • "Have tariffs impacted your component costs this year?"
  • "Do you ever pay expedite fees to hit deadlines?"

Compliance Problems:

  • "Have you had shipments delayed due to missing documentation?"
  • "How much time does your team spend on customs paperwork?"

  • 140+ total dials (20/day)

Week 2: Progression (Days 8-14)

Weekly Goal

Master Implication & Need-Payoff questions. Make pain real and let them sell themselves.

Days 8-10: Add Implication Questions

‚ö†Ô∏è This is where amateur reps fail. They find the problem but don't make it HURT.
The Formula
  1. They tell you the problem
  2. You ask about the CONSEQUENCES
  3. They convince themselves it's urgent

Example:

Problem: "We sometimes miss deadlines due to parts shortages."

‚ùå Bad response: "We can help with that! We stock parts."

‚úÖ Good response: "That's frustrating. What happens when you miss a deadline? How does your customer react?"
Core Implication Questions

Financial Implications:

  • "When production stops, what does that cost you per day?"
  • "If tariffs keep rising, how does that affect your margins?"

Operational Implications:

  • "When you miss a dock date, what's the cascade effect?"
  • "If you're managing 10 suppliers, how much admin time does that consume?"

Relationship Implications:

  • "If you keep missing deadlines, how does that affect customer relationships?"
  • "Have you lost bids because competitors could guarantee better lead times?"
Success Indicator: The prospect says "Wow, I never thought about it that way" or "We really need to fix this."

Days 11-14: Complete SPIN Sequence

Need-Payoff Questions

After they've articulated implications, ask them to envision the solution:

"So if I'm hearing you right, missing dock dates is causing real problems with your OEM customers. What would it mean to your business if you could guarantee parts availability?"

Specific to Summon:

  • "If we stocked 12 months of critical parts at locked prices, would that help?"
  • "If we handled all customs paperwork, would that free up your team?"
  • "Would having a partner who sources from ANY manufacturer give you leverage?"
The Full SPIN Call (10-15 minutes)

Opening: "Hi [Name], I help companies solve supply chain challenges. Do you have 5 minutes?"

Situation (2 min): "Before I explain what we do, can I ask about your current sourcing process?"

Problem (2 min): "A lot of companies mention challenges with [X]. Is that something you deal with?"

Implication (2 min): "When that happens, what's the impact on production/costs/customers?"

Need-Payoff (2 min): "If we could solve that, how would that change things for you?"

Close: "Based on what you've shared, I think we can help. Can I send you a proposal?"

Week 2 Graduation Requirements

  • 500+ total dials (cumulative)
  • 50+ conversations
  • 20+ full SPIN sequences executed
  • Can ask 3+ Implication questions that land
  • Prospects articulate Need-Payoff (not you)
  • 10+ prospects agreed to next step

üè≠ Industry-Specific Frameworks

CEM/EMS Contract Manufacturers

What they do: Build products for other companies

Their pain: Tight schedules, thin margins, line shutdowns

What they care about: Speed, locked pricing, stocking programs

Situation Questions:

  • "How many production lines are you running?"
  • "What's your typical lead time from PO to dock date?"
  • "Walk me through what happens when you get a hot shortage."

Problem Questions:

  • "When a part goes on allocation, what happens to your schedule?"
  • "How often do shortages cause you to shut down a line?"
  • "Have you ever missed a dock date and paid penalties?"

Implication Questions:

  • "When a line shuts down, what does that cost per hour?"
  • "If you miss dock dates, how does that affect your OEM relationships?"
  • "When you pay expedite fees regularly, how does that eat into margins?"

Need-Payoff Questions:

  • "If we stocked 6-12 months of critical parts at locked prices, would that help?"
  • "What would it mean if you never paid expedite fees again?"
OEM Original Equipment Manufacturers

What they do: Design and build their own products

Their pain: Long lifecycles, obsolescence, engineering approval

What they care about: Obsolete parts, alternates, engineering support

Situation Questions:

  • "What's a typical product lifecycle for you?"
  • "Walk me through what happens when a component goes EOL."

Problem Questions:

  • "How often do parts go EOL mid-lifecycle?"
  • "Have you had to redesign a board because you couldn't find an alternate?"

Implication Questions:

  • "When you redesign due to obsolescence, what does that cost?"
  • "If launches get delayed, how does that affect revenue?"

Need-Payoff Questions:

  • "If we provided pre-vetted alternates with full specs, would that speed up approval?"
  • "What if you had a partner who specializes in obsolete parts?"
Transit/Public Transit Authorities & Public Sector

What they do: Government agencies, transportation, utilities

Their pain: Legacy systems (20-60 years old), strict compliance

What they care about: Documentation, traceability, PPAP, certs

Situation Questions:

  • "How old is your fleet or infrastructure?"
  • "What compliance documentation do you require from suppliers?"

Problem Questions:

  • "When you need parts for 30-year-old equipment, how easy is it to find them?"
  • "Do suppliers typically provide all the documentation you need?"

Implication Questions:

  • "When vehicles are out of service, what's the impact on ridership?"
  • "How does public perception change when service is disrupted?"

Need-Payoff Questions:

  • "If we provided full PPAP and traceability for every part, would that reduce audit risk?"
  • "What if you had a supplier who specializes in obsolete transit parts?"

üí¨ Objection Handling via SPIN

üí° Key Principle: Don't "overcome" objections. Use SPIN to reframe the conversation.
"We only buy from authorized distributors"

‚ùå Old Way (Defensive)

"We're not a broker! We're an independent distributor with full traceability..."

‚úÖ SPIN Way (Curious)

Situation: "That makes sense. Do your authorized distributors carry EVERY manufacturer you need?"

Problem: "What happens when you need one they don't support?"

Implication: "So instead of one distributor, you're managing how many extra relationships?"

Need-Payoff: "If you had one partner who could source ANY manufacturer with full traceability, would that simplify your supply chain?"
"Your prices are higher"

‚ùå Old Way

"Let me check with my manager and see if I can do better."

‚úÖ SPIN Way

Situation: "Can I ask - what does that other quote include? Is it apples-to-apples on lead time?"

Problem: "If you can't get parts for 12 weeks, what happens?"

Implication: "What's the cost of missing that dock date? If the penalty is 10% and you save 15% on parts but lose 10% on penalty, are you really saving?"

Need-Payoff: "You're not buying parts, you're buying on-time delivery. If we can guarantee you'll hit your dock date, is that worth a premium?"
"We're all set with our current suppliers"

‚ùå Old Way

"Well, if anything changes, keep us in mind!"

‚úÖ SPIN Way

Situation: "That's great. Are those suppliers able to handle EVERYTHING, or do you ever have gaps?"

Problem: "So when that happens, what do you do?"

Implication: "When you're scrambling or redesigning, how does that affect timeline and costs?"

Need-Payoff: "Would it be helpful to have a backup partner who specializes in those hard-to-find, gap-filler parts?"
"Just send me an email"

‚ùå Old Way

"Sure, what's your email?" [sends generic email that gets ignored]

‚úÖ SPIN Way

Situation: "Happy to. Before I send something generic, can I ask one quick question? What's your biggest sourcing challenge right now?"

Problem: "How often does that come up?"

Implication: "When you can't find those parts, what happens?"

Need-Payoff: "If I send you examples of how we've solved that, would that be useful? And can I follow up Thursday to discuss?"

The 3-Second Rule

After you ask a question, count to 3 before speaking again.

Most reps fill silence immediately. Great reps let silence work for them.
